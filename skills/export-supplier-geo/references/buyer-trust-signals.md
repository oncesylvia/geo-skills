# Overseas buyer trust signals — the checklist

A cross-border buyer (and their AI) is asking: *is this supplier real, capable,
and safe to order from?* Surface real proof of each, where AI and buyers can read
it (trade-platform profile, English site, LinkedIn). Only claim what's true —
forged signals are fraud and one check ends the deal.

## Identity & legitimacy
- [ ] Registered company name + **business registration / license** number.
- [ ] **Years in business**; real founding date.
- [ ] Real **address**; factory/office **photos and video** (a factory tour video
      is powerful and AI-surfaceable).
- [ ] Platform **verified / gold / assessed-supplier** status (Alibaba Verified,
      Made-in-China Audited, etc.) where genuinely held.

## Certifications & compliance (list those that apply)
- [ ] Quality: **ISO 9001**, IATF 16949 (auto), ISO 13485 (medical)…
- [ ] Product/market: **CE, RoHS, REACH** (EU); **FDA** (US food/medical);
      UL, FCC; food/cosmetic certs as relevant.
- [ ] Social/ethical audits: **BSCI, Sedex/SMETA, WRAP**, factory audit reports.
- [ ] State each clearly with issuing body + scope; offer to share certificates.

## Capability & terms (what buyers screen on)
- [ ] Production **capacity**, main **equipment**, factory size.
- [ ] **MOQ**, **lead time**, sampling, customization/OEM/ODM.
- [ ] **QC process** (incoming/in-process/final inspection), defect handling.
- [ ] **Main export markets** and notable clients/case references (with permission).
- [ ] Payment terms, trade assurance / escrow options.

## Third-party proof
- [ ] **Buyer reviews / ratings** on trade platforms (real ones).
- [ ] Trade-assurance / transaction history where shown.
- [ ] Industry directory listings; trade-show participation.
- [ ] LinkedIn company page + real team profiles.

## Credibility of presentation
- [ ] **Professional English** (not raw machine translation — bad English reads
      as a scam risk to buyers and AI).
- [ ] Clear, fast inquiry/RFQ path; responsive contact; time zone/hours stated.
- [ ] Consistent company name/entity across every platform.

## Where to surface it (so AI can read it)
- Trade-platform "Company Profile" / "About" sections — fill completely.
- Your English site: an "About / Why us / Certifications / Factory" page,
  answer-first, with the certs and proof in **text** (not only images).
- LinkedIn company page.
- Keep all of it consistent so AI ties it to one trustworthy entity.

## The line
Surface only **real** certifications, audits, registration, and capabilities.
Fabricated trust signals are fraud, get you blacklisted from platforms, and
collapse the moment a buyer verifies. Build trust on what's true.
