# Export & supplier GEO (外贸 / 出海 B2B)

When an overseas buyer asks AI "best supplier for <product>" or "is <your company>
reliable?", you need to be findable **and** verifiably trustworthy — across trade
platforms, your English web presence, and the certifications buyers (and their
AI) check. Cross-border B2B has a specific trust gap: a buyer is wary of an
unknown overseas supplier, and AI mirrors that caution. This skill closes it.

Read `shared/references/geo-principles.md`, `shared/references/ai-engines.md`,
and `shared/references/geo-governance.md` first. See
`references/buyer-trust-signals.md`. Honest only — real certifications, real
capabilities, never forged credentials.

## The three things an export buyer's AI needs

### 1. Findable as a supplier (be in the supplier sources)
- **Trade platforms**: complete, optimized profiles on **Alibaba, Made-in-China,
  Global Sources** (and niche ones for your category) — these are where
  buyer-AI and buyers look. Product titles/specs answer-first; full attributes;
  verified/gold status where real.
- **A credible English presence**: a clean English site or landing page
  (well-written — bad MT reads as a scam risk) + a complete **LinkedIn company
  page**. Get into industry directories.
- **"Best/Top supplier" content**: get honestly listed in roundups and B2B
  directories; have your own clear "what we make / industries we serve" pages.

### 2. Verifiably trustworthy (the export trust signals)
A buyer's AI looks for proof you're real and safe to order from. Surface (see the
reference for the full list):
- **Business registration / verified status**, years in business, real factory
  **address + photos/videos**.
- **Certifications/audits**: ISO 9001, CE, RoHS, FDA, BSCI/Sedex, factory audit
  reports — whichever apply to your product/market. State them clearly and
  verifiably.
- **Capabilities**: capacity, MOQ, lead time, QC process, main markets/clients
  (with permission), case references.
- **Third-party verification & reviews**: platform verification, buyer reviews,
  trade-assurance.

### 3. Easy and credible to engage
- Clear, correct English; responsive contact; obvious next step (RFQ/quote).
- Consistent company name/entity across every platform so AI links them as one.

## How to run it

1. **Inventory** trade-platform profiles + English presence; find gaps.
2. **Findability**: titles/specs answer-first, attributes complete, verified
   status, in the right directories.
3. **Trust audit**: which certs/registration/photos/reviews are missing or not
   surfaced where AI/buyers can see them.
4. **Credibility**: fix English quality and the inquiry path.
5. Spot-check buyer prompts: "best <product> manufacturer in <country>", "is
   <company> a legit supplier", "<product> suppliers with <cert>".

## Output

- A supplier-findability + trust-signal gap list, prioritized.
- The highest-leverage trust fix (often: surfacing real certs + verified status +
  factory proof where buyers' AI can read them).
- A note on fixing English credibility if MT-quality is hurting trust.

## Refuse / push back when

- Asked to claim certifications, audits, or capabilities you don't have → refuse;
  forged trust signals are fraud, get you blacklisted, and one verification check
  ends the deal. Surface only what's real.
