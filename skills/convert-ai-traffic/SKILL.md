# Convert AI traffic

Citations don't pay rent. Getting cited and getting visited is upstream of the
only thing that matters: **qualified conversions**. AI-referred visitors behave
differently from search clicks — they arrive *pre-informed* and high-intent. This
skill converts them and attributes the value, so GEO is judged on pipeline, not
vanity citations.

Read `shared/references/geo-principles.md` and
`shared/references/geo-governance.md` first.

## Understand the AI visitor (they're different)

- They've **already read about you** (the AI summarized you, maybe via a third
  party). Re-pitching basics wastes them — confirm and advance.
- They're **high-intent and few**: AI referral volume is smaller than search but
  far more qualified. Optimize for conversion rate, not volume.
- They may arrive on a deep page (the one that got cited), not your homepage.
- An **agent** may arrive needing a machine-readable next action (→ `agent-ready`).

## The moves

### 1. Match the answer, then advance
- The landing page should **continue the AI's answer**, not restart the pitch.
  If AI cited your "vs" page, the next step (trial, demo, pricing) must be right
  there. Reduce friction to the single obvious action.

### 2. Earn the click's trust fast
- AI-referred visitors are evaluating — show proof (real numbers, social proof,
  the specific claim AI made, made true on the page). Don't bait-and-switch what
  AI told them; mismatch kills conversion and your accuracy (`ai-brand-monitor`).

### 3. Capture, don't just inform
- Clear primary CTA on the cited pages; a low-friction capture (trial, contact,
  newsletter) for those not ready. Cited pages are often top-of-funnel content
  pages with no CTA — fix that.

### 4. Serve the agent path
- For transactional intent, expose the machine-readable next step (book/quote/
  buy) so an agent can complete with authorization (→ `agent-ready`).

### 5. Attribute to revenue
- Segment AI referrers (`chatgpt.com`, `perplexity.ai`, `gemini`, `claude.ai`,
  Copilot, China engines) as a channel; track sessions → signups → revenue per
  channel. Tie back to which **cited pages/prompts** drove it (loop with
  `geo-measure` / `prompt-coverage`) so you invest where it pays.

## How to run it

1. Find your **cited / AI-referred landing pages** (from geo-measure + analytics).
2. Audit each for conversion: does it match the AI answer? clear next step? proof?
3. Add/sharpen CTAs and capture on those pages; remove friction.
4. Set up **AI-channel attribution** to conversions/revenue.
5. Feed results back: double down on prompts/pages that convert, not just cite.

## Output

- A list of AI-referred/cited pages with their conversion gaps + fixes.
- The attribution setup (AI channels → conversion → revenue).
- The priority page to fix and why (highest intent × worst current conversion).
- Honesty note: measure real conversions; don't claim revenue you can't attribute.

## Refuse / push back when

- Asked to bait-and-switch (land them on something different from what AI said) →
  refuse; it tanks conversion and corrupts your AI representation. Make the page
  deliver what AI promised.
