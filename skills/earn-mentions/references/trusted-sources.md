# Trusted off-site sources — the playbook per source

Earn presence, never fake it. Each source below includes the honest way in and
the line not to cross.

## Reddit (highest cross-engine weight)
- **Why**: data deals + real discussion → heavily cited by ChatGPT, Google,
  Perplexity.
- **Earn it**: find the subreddits your buyers actually use; answer questions
  with genuine expertise over weeks; share real experience; mention your product
  only where relevant and **disclose** you're the maker.
- **Don't**: drop links cold, run multiple accounts, buy upvotes. Reddit removes
  it and the engines learn to distrust thin/promotional threads.

## Wikidata (quick entity win)
- **Why**: structured entity graph engines use to know what your brand *is*.
- **Earn it**: create a Wikidata item for your company with accurate properties
  (official site, founded, industry, social profiles). Achievable for most
  companies; must be factual and verifiable.
- **Link back**: add those profile URLs to your site's `Organization` schema
  `sameAs` so the identity connects.

## Wikipedia (hard — only if genuinely notable)
- **Why**: strong entity + trust signal.
- **Earn it**: requires real **notability** — significant independent coverage.
  If you don't have it, don't force a page (it'll be deleted and looks bad).
  Build notability first (press, milestones), then a neutral editor may create it.
- **Don't**: pay for a covert page or write a promotional autobiography.

## Review & directory sites
- **Which**: G2, Capterra, TrustRadius, Product Hunt, Trustpilot, plus niche
  directories for your category.
- **Earn it**: claim/create your listing with complete, accurate info; ask **real
  customers** for honest reviews (a neutral ask is fine).
- **Don't**: write or incentivize fake reviews — illegal in many places and a
  trust-killer.

## Roundups / "best X" listicles
- **Why**: engines lift "best X for Y" lists directly.
- **Earn it**: find current roundups for your category; email the author with a
  specific, honest reason you belong (a real differentiator, not a bribe). Or
  publish **your own genuinely fair** roundup that includes competitors honestly
  — useful content that itself gets cited.
- **Don't**: pay for placement disguised as editorial without disclosure.

## YouTube
- **Why**: increasingly surfaced and cited.
- **Earn it**: a real demo, tutorial, or explainer; clear titles matching
  questions people ask; accurate description with links.

## Press & guest content
- **Earn it**: genuine story angles, data you can share, guest posts on credible
  industry sites. Earned coverage compounds as an entity/trust signal.

## China sources (出海/国内)
- **知乎** (Reddit-like weight), **小红书**, **B站** (≈ YouTube), **微信公众号**,
  **百度百科** (Baike — entity), industry媒体/榜单.
- Same rule: real value, real reviews, real inclusion. 别买水军/刷量——平台和
  引擎都在识别。

## The disclosure & ethics checklist
- [ ] Affiliation disclosed everywhere you participate as the maker.
- [ ] No fake/incentivized reviews; only neutral asks to real users.
- [ ] No sockpuppets, bought upvotes, or paid placement disguised as organic.
- [ ] Everything stated is true and verifiable.
- [ ] `sameAs` in your schema links your verified profiles back to your site.
