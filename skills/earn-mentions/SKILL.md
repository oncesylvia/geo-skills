# Earn mentions (the off-site half of GEO)

Optimizing your own site is only half of GEO. Across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and
Google, a huge share of citations come from **third-party sources the engine
trusts** — Reddit, Wikipedia, review sites, roundups. If your site is perfect but
you're invisible in those, AI still won't cite you. This skill builds that
presence — honestly.

Read `shared/references/geo-principles.md` and `shared/references/ai-engines.md`
first. The line here is bright: **no fake reviews, no astroturfing, no paid
manipulation disguised as organic.** That's both against platform rules and
exactly what erodes the trust you're trying to build.

## The sources engines weight (and how to earn each)

See `references/trusted-sources.md` for the playbook per source. In short:

- **Reddit** — hugely weighted. Be genuinely useful in the subreddits your buyers
  live in; answer questions, share real experience, disclose affiliation. Don't
  spam links or sockpuppet — Reddit nukes it and engines distrust it.
- **Wikipedia / Wikidata** — entity grounding. A **Wikidata** item is achievable
  for most companies and helps engines know what your brand *is*. Wikipedia
  proper requires genuine notability (independent coverage) — don't force it.
- **Review & directory sites** — G2, Capterra, Product Hunt, Trustpilot, niche
  directories. Get listed; ask real customers for honest reviews (asking is
  fine; scripting fake ones is not).
- **Roundups / listicles** — "best X for Y" posts. Get **honestly** included by
  reaching out to authors with a real reason you belong; or publish your own
  genuinely fair roundup that includes competitors.
- **YouTube** — increasingly surfaced; a real demo/explainer video can become a
  cited source.
- **Reputable press & docs** — earned coverage, guest posts on credible sites,
  great public docs.

## How to run it

1. **Find where the citations come from**: for your target questions, look at
   what sources the engines actually cite today (often Reddit threads, a G2
   page, a listicle). Those are the rooms you need to be in. (Pairs with
   `citation-gap`.)
2. **Prioritize by effort × trust**: Wikidata item and key directory listings are
   quick wins; Reddit presence is a slow burn; Wikipedia is hard. Sequence
   accordingly.
3. **Earn, don't fake**: contribute real value, get real reviews, pitch real
   inclusion. Disclose affiliation everywhere required.
4. **Make yourself easy to cite**: ensure your entity page and `sameAs` links
   (schema) connect your site to these profiles so engines link the identity.

## Output

- A ranked list of off-site sources to earn, with the specific action for each.
- Quick wins first (Wikidata, directories), slow burns flagged (Reddit, Wikipedia).
- A disclosure/ethics checklist so nothing crosses into astroturfing.

## Refuse / push back when

- Asked to write fake reviews, run sockpuppets, buy mass upvotes, or astroturf →
  refuse. It violates platform policy, gets detected, and destroys the
  credibility that earns citations. Offer the honest version instead.
