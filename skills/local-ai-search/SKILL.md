# Local AI search

When buyers ask AI "best <service> near me" or "<service> in <city>", the engines
lean on **local signals** — Google Business Profile, map data, local directories,
NAP consistency, reviews — not just your website. This skill makes a
location-based business citable for local AI queries (and AI Overviews / Gemini /
ChatGPT local results), which the global GEO skills don't cover.

Read `shared/references/geo-principles.md` and `shared/references/ai-engines.md`
first. See `references/local-checklist.md`. Honest only: real listings, real
reviews — no fake locations or review fraud.

## The local signals that matter

### 1. Google Business Profile (the anchor)
- Claimed, verified, **complete**: correct categories, hours, service area,
  description, photos, attributes, products/services.
- Keep it **current** — wrong hours/closed status is the classic local failure.
- Use Posts, Q&A, and respond to reviews.

### 2. NAP consistency (name / address / phone)
- Identical NAP everywhere — your site, GBP, Apple/Bing Maps, directories, social.
- Inconsistencies confuse engines about which entity/location is real and split
  your authority. Audit and fix mismatches.

### 3. Local schema & pages
- `LocalBusiness` (or specific subtype) schema with address, geo, hours, `sameAs`.
- **Service-area / city pages** for each location or area you serve — genuinely
  useful, not doorway spam.

### 4. Local directories & reviews
- Be listed accurately on Apple Maps, Bing Places, and the **directories your
  industry uses** (Yelp, TripAdvisor, niche ones).
- Earn **real reviews** with steady volume and responses; review quality/recency
  is a strong local trust signal. Never buy or fake reviews.

## How to run it

1. **GBP audit**: claimed, complete, accurate, current?
2. **NAP audit**: pull your NAP across all surfaces — list mismatches to fix.
3. **Schema + pages**: LocalBusiness schema present? a real page per location/area?
4. **Listings + reviews**: on the key local platforms? review volume/recency/
   responses healthy?
5. Spot-check the local prompts ("best <service> in <city>", "<service> near me")
   across engines; see who's cited.

## Output

- A local readiness checklist with pass/fail.
- The NAP inconsistencies to fix (highest-leverage local fix).
- Priority moves: GBP completeness → NAP → schema/pages → reviews.

## Refuse / push back when

- Asked to create fake locations, keyword-stuff the business name, or buy/fake
  reviews → refuse; it's against platform policy, risks suspension, and is fraud.
