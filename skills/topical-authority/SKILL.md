# Topical authority & E-E-A-T

Engines preferentially cite sources they judge **authoritative on a topic** —
which is why a thin site with one good page loses to a competitor with deep,
interlinked coverage and visible expertise. This skill builds that authority:
topical depth plus credible authorship. It's the difference between being cited
once and being the **default** source.

Read `shared/references/geo-principles.md` and `shared/references/ai-engines.md`
first. See `references/clusters-and-eeat.md` for the framework. Honest only:
authority is earned with real depth and real expertise, never faked credentials.

## Two pillars

### 1. Topical depth (content clusters)
Cover a topic comprehensively so engines see breadth + depth:
- A **pillar page** (the definitive overview of the topic) +
- **Supporting pages** answering every sub-question/long-tail around it, all
  **interlinked** back to the pillar and each other.
- This signals "this site covers <topic> thoroughly" — far stronger than scattered
  one-offs. Plan clusters from your `prompt-coverage` map.

### 2. Demonstrated expertise (E-E-A-T)
Engines weigh **Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust**:
- **Real authors** with bylines, credentials, and bio pages (`Person`/`author`
  schema). "Who wrote this and why should I trust them" matters.
- **First-hand experience** signals: original data, real screenshots, "we tested",
  case studies — not generic rehash.
- **Citations to primary sources**; accurate, current facts.
- **Trust pages**: clear About, Contact, policies, a real org behind it
  (`Organization` schema, consistent entity).
- A consistent, well-defined **entity** across the web (ties to `earn-mentions`
  Wikidata + `ai-brand-monitor`).

## How to run it

1. Pick the **core topic(s)** you must own (from prompt-coverage / your category).
2. Design the **cluster**: pillar + the supporting pages that cover it fully;
   map internal links.
3. Add/upgrade **authorship & trust signals**: bylines, bios, credentials,
   sources, About/Contact, schema.
4. Fill gaps with `citable-pages` / `answer-ready-content`; interlink everything.
5. Reassess over time — authority compounds; it's a campaign, not a one-page fix.

## Output

- The cluster plan (pillar + supporting pages + internal-link map) for the topic.
- An E-E-A-T gap list (missing authors, bios, sources, trust pages, schema).
- The priority moves, sequenced (depth + credibility compound together).

中文同样吃话题权威:做中文内容簇 + 真实作者署名 + 行业背书。国内信任信号还包括
**公众号矩阵、知乎专栏、行业媒体专栏**。分语言各建一套权威,别只翻译。

## Refuse / push back when

- Asked to fabricate author credentials, fake "expert reviewed" badges, or
  invent data/case studies → refuse; faked E-E-A-T is detectable and destroys the
  trust you're building. Build real expertise signals.
